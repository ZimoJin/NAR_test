USER Cloning: Uracil-Enabled Sticky-End Primer Design for Seamless Assembly

Abstract: The USER cloning module designs primers for uracil-specific excision reagent (USER) assembly. It supports both auto-generated U strategies and workflows where the user provides U-containing primers, and it reports junction overlaps and QC metrics to facilitate reliable, directional cloning for single- and multi-insert assemblies.

Introduction

USER cloning is a ligation-independent method that creates long, defined overhangs by incorporating a uracil residue into PCR primers and treating amplicons with a USER enzyme mix. Excision at the uracil position generates complementary sticky ends that anneal efficiently and promote directional assembly.

Inputs

  • Vector sequence: Circular plasmid sequence for linearization and junction context.
  • Insert sequence(s): One or more fragments (FASTA supported) in the intended assembly order.
  • Vector linearization primers: Provided without U (auto-U mode) or with U (user-defined U mode), depending on workflow.
  • Constraints: Target overlap length/Tm and target core Tm for binding regions.

Design Strategy

Junction-centered overlap selection

The module defines junctions between adjacent fragments (vector–insert1, insert–insert, and insertN–vector). For each junction, it searches for candidate overlaps consistent with USER-compatible patterns and selects an overlap based on proximity to the junction and thermodynamic preferences.

Uracil placement

In auto-U mode, the module places a uracil at a defined position within the overlap (commonly by selecting an overlap ending in T and substituting the terminal T with U). In user-defined mode, U-containing vector primers are treated as fixed inputs, and the module derives corresponding insert overhangs from the provided overlaps.

Core binding regions

After selecting an overlap, the 3′ core binding region is designed on the appropriate side of the overlap boundary to avoid overlap/core duplication. Core sequences are sized to match a target Tm and then screened by the shared QC engine.

Multi-insert assemblies

For multiple inserts, each junction is computed independently and primers are produced per insert (Insert1-F/R, Insert2-F/R, etc.), enabling a consistent naming scheme and transparent junction-by-junction verification.

Outputs

  • Primer sets: Vector primers and per-insert primer pairs for single- and multi-insert workflows.
  • Overlap table: Junction overlaps, predicted overlap Tm, and GC% for quick validation.
  • QC report: Hairpin/dimer summaries for each primer and key primer pairs.

Limitations and Practical Notes

  • Overlaps are recommended computationally; users should confirm that the resulting cleavage/annealing preserves reading frame and does not introduce unwanted sequence changes.
  • Templates with limited USER-compatible motifs near junctions may force fallback overlaps; the module flags such cases in warnings.
  • Experimental success also depends on enzyme mix, polymerase, and purification strategy; these factors are outside the scope of primer design.
